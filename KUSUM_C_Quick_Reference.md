## ⚠️ COMMON OBJECTIONS & ANSWERS

### Objection 1: "Too expensive for me"
**Answer:** You pay only ₹1.2L (40%) after 60% subsidy. Bank loan available for your share. Monthly benefit (₹5,000) is more than EMI (₹2,500).

### Objection 2: "What if it doesn't work in monsoon?"
**Answer:** System works in diffused light. On cloudy days, grid power supplements. You still have net metering benefits and annual settlement.

### Objection 3: "Maintenance will be costly"
**Answer:** Very minimal maintenance - just cleaning once a month. Annual cost only ₹2,000-5,000. Your monthly benefit is ₹5,000+.

### Objection 4: "I don't have cash"
**Answer:** That's why bank loans exist! You can get 90% of your contribution (36% of total) as loan. EMI: ₹2,500, Your benefit: ₹5,000. Net gain: ₹2,500/month!

### Objection 5: "Scheme might end"
**Answer:** Yes! That's why you must act NOW. Scheme valid only till March 2026. After that, no 60% subsidy guarantee.

### Objection 6: "I have small land"
**Answer:** Perfect! Even 300 sq.ft is enough for 3 kW system. You'll still get subsidy and benefits proportionally.

## 🔧 TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE

**Issue: Farmer says "I'll think about it"**
- **Solution:** Create urgency. "Scheme ends March 2026. Processing takes 3-4 months. Last date to apply is essentially June 2025."

**Issue: Concerned about maintenance**
- **Solution:** Show maintenance checklist. Compare annual maintenance cost (₹3,000) vs annual benefit (₹60,000).

**Issue: "My neighbor said it doesn't work"**
- **Solution:** Get details. Usually installation quality issue or wrong vendor. Show successful installations in same area.

**Issue: Bank loan rejection**
- **Solution:** Try different bank. NABARD has special schemes. Help with documentation.

**Issue: DISCOM delaying feasibility**
- **Solution:** Escalate to APNREDC. Follow up weekly. Keep documentation ready.

## 🚨 RED FLAGS TO WATCH

**Vendor Red Flags:**
- Not in MNRE empaneled list
- Asking 100% advance payment

- Promising >60% subsidy
- Very cheap quotations (quality concern)

**Farmer Red Flags:**
- Land disputes pending
- Electricity bill payment defaults
- Unrealistic expectations
- Not decision maker
- Looking for shortcuts
